# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

**Document ID:** PTO-2025-001 **Policy Area:** Time Off and Leave **Version:** 2.4 **Effective Date:** January 1, 2025 **Last Reviewed:** December 10, 2024 **Owner:** Human Resources Department

---

## 1\. Policy Overview

### 1.1 Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the accrual, use, and management of Paid Time Off (PTO) for eligible employees. PTO provides employees with paid time away from work for vacation, personal needs, illness, family obligations, and other reasons. The policy is designed to balance employee flexibility with the operational needs of the Company.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to all regular full-time and part-time employees of the Company. Temporary employees, seasonal employees, and independent contractors are not eligible for PTO under this policy. Employees in states with separate paid sick leave requirements are subject to additional provisions described in the State-Specific Provisions section.

### 1.3 Policy Statement

The Company combines vacation, personal, and sick leave into a single PTO bank, allowing employees flexibility in how they use their paid time off. PTO is a benefit and a privilege, not a guaranteed right, and is subject to the terms of this policy.

---

## 2\. Eligibility

### 2.1 Full-Time Employees

Regular full-time employees scheduled to work 30 or more hours per week are eligible to begin accruing PTO on their first day of employment. PTO may be used after the completion of 90 days of continuous employment, except where state law requires earlier availability.

### 2.2 Part-Time Employees

Regular part-time employees scheduled to work between 20 and 29 hours per week accrue PTO on a prorated basis based on their scheduled hours. Part-time employees scheduled to work fewer than 20 hours per week are not eligible for PTO except where required by state law.

### 2.3 Ineligible Categories

The following categories of workers are not eligible for PTO under this policy: temporary employees, seasonal employees, independent contractors, interns, and employees on unpaid leaves of absence. Eligibility is determined based on classification at the time PTO is accrued or used.

---

## 3\. PTO Accrual

### 3.1 Accrual Rates by Tenure

Full-time employees accrue PTO based on years of continuous service. Years of service are calculated from the employee's most recent hire date. The accrual schedule is as follows:

For employees with less than one year of service, PTO accrues at a rate of 3.08 hours per pay period, equivalent to 80 hours or 10 days annually. For employees with one to four years of service, PTO accrues at 4.62 hours per pay period, equivalent to 120 hours or 15 days annually. For employees with five to nine years of service, PTO accrues at 6.15 hours per pay period, equivalent to 160 hours or 20 days annually. For employees with ten or more years of service, PTO accrues at 7.69 hours per pay period, equivalent to 200 hours or 25 days annually.

### 3.2 Accrual Tier Transitions

Employees move to the next accrual tier on the first pay period following their service anniversary. For example, an employee hired on March 15, 2024 will move from the 0–1 year tier to the 1–4 year tier on the pay period that includes March 15, 2025\.

### 3.3 Part-Time Accrual

Part-time employees accrue PTO at a prorated rate based on their scheduled hours. The proration is calculated by dividing the employee's scheduled weekly hours by 40 and applying that percentage to the full-time accrual rate.

### 3.4 Accrual During Leave

PTO continues to accrue during paid leaves of absence, including PTO usage and short-term disability. PTO does not accrue during unpaid leaves of absence, including unpaid FMLA leave, unpaid personal leave, or unpaid disciplinary suspension. Accrual resumes upon return to active employment.

### 3.5 Maximum Accrual Cap

The maximum PTO balance an employee may accumulate is 1.5 times the employee's annual accrual rate. Once the cap is reached, no additional PTO is accrued until the balance is reduced through use. The Company does not pay employees for PTO they were unable to accrue due to reaching the cap.

---

## 4\. Using PTO

### 4.1 Requesting PTO

PTO requests must be submitted through the Company's time-off request system. Employees should request PTO as far in advance as possible. For planned absences, requests should be submitted at least two weeks in advance for periods of two days or more, and at least 24 hours in advance for single-day absences.

### 4.2 Approval Process

PTO requests are subject to manager approval based on business needs, staffing requirements, and existing schedule conflicts. Managers will respond to PTO requests within five business days of submission. Approval is not guaranteed, and managers may deny requests due to operational requirements.

### 4.3 Unscheduled PTO

Unscheduled PTO use, such as for illness or emergencies, must be reported to the employee's manager as soon as possible, and no later than 30 minutes after the employee's scheduled start time. Excessive unscheduled PTO use may be addressed through performance management.

### 4.4 Minimum and Maximum Increments

PTO may be used in minimum increments of one hour. There is no maximum length of PTO that may be requested, but extended absences of two weeks or more require additional advance notice and may be subject to enhanced approval requirements.

### 4.5 Holidays During PTO

Company-observed holidays that fall during a PTO period are paid as holidays and do not count against the employee's PTO balance. For example, an employee taking PTO during the week of Thanksgiving will not be charged PTO for the Thanksgiving holiday.

### 4.6 PTO Use Order

PTO is used in the order it was accrued. When state-mandated paid sick leave is provided in addition to general PTO, the state-mandated sick leave is generally used first for qualifying purposes, in accordance with state law.

## 5\. Carryover and Forfeiture

### 5.1 Annual Carryover

Employees may carry over up to 40 hours of unused PTO into the following calendar year. The carryover limit applies to the PTO balance as of December 31 of each year. PTO in excess of the carryover limit is forfeited unless state law prohibits forfeiture.

### 5.2 Carryover Use Deadline

Carried-over PTO must be used by March 31 of the following year. PTO carried over but not used by this deadline is forfeited, except where state law prohibits forfeiture.

### 5.3 State Law Variations

In states that prohibit "use it or lose it" PTO policies, accrued and unused PTO does not expire and is not forfeited. In these states, the maximum accrual cap operates to limit total PTO accumulation rather than annual forfeiture. Employees in such states should refer to the State-Specific Provisions section.

### 5.4 Cash-Out at Year-End

The Company does not provide a year-end cash-out option for unused PTO above the carryover limit. Employees are encouraged to plan their PTO use throughout the year to avoid forfeiture.

---

## 6\. PTO and Other Leave Interactions

### 6.1 PTO and FMLA Leave

When an employee takes leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA), the Company requires the use of accrued PTO concurrently with FMLA leave. This means FMLA leave and PTO run simultaneously, and the employee receives pay from PTO while protected under FMLA. Once PTO is exhausted, the remainder of the FMLA leave is unpaid.

### 6.2 PTO and Disability Leave

Employees on short-term or long-term disability leave may use accrued PTO to supplement disability benefits up to 100% of regular pay. PTO use during disability leave is voluntary except where required by Company policy or state law.

### 6.3 PTO and Workers' Compensation

Employees receiving workers' compensation benefits may elect to use accrued PTO to supplement workers' compensation payments up to 100% of regular pay. Total compensation from workers' compensation and PTO may not exceed regular pay.

### 6.4 PTO and Bereavement Leave

Employees who require bereavement leave beyond the Company-provided bereavement days may use accrued PTO. Bereavement leave does not reduce the PTO balance for the days specifically designated as paid bereavement leave under the Employee Handbook.

### 6.5 PTO and Jury Duty

Employees on jury duty receive paid leave for the duration of their service and are not required to use PTO. Employees may, however, elect to use PTO if jury duty pay arrangements do not cover the full period of absence.

---

## 7\. PTO at Separation

### 7.1 Payout of Unused PTO

Upon voluntary or involuntary separation from employment, employees will be paid for accrued, unused PTO in their final paycheck, in accordance with applicable state law. The payout is calculated at the employee's regular rate of pay at the time of separation.

### 7.2 Payout Limitations

In states that do not require PTO payout at termination, the Company's practice is to pay accrued, unused PTO upon voluntary resignation when the employee has provided at least two weeks' notice. Employees who resign without providing notice or who are terminated for misconduct may forfeit unused PTO, where permitted by state law.

### 7.3 Resignation During PTO Period

Employees may not use PTO to extend their employment date for purposes of benefits eligibility, vesting, or service credit. The last day of employment is the last day worked, regardless of PTO that would have been taken thereafter.

### 7.4 Reinstatement of PTO

Former employees rehired within 90 days of separation will have any PTO balance paid out at separation reinstated, and previous service credit will be applied for accrual rate purposes. Employees rehired after 90 days will be treated as new hires for PTO purposes.

## 8\. PTO Donation Program

### 8.1 Program Overview

The Company maintains a voluntary PTO donation program that allows employees to donate accrued PTO to colleagues facing serious medical conditions or family emergencies. Donations are voluntary and confidential.

### 8.2 Eligibility to Donate

Employees with a PTO balance of at least 80 hours may donate up to 40 hours of PTO per calendar year. Donations are made in eight-hour increments. Donated PTO is converted to dollar value based on the donor's hourly rate and credited to the recipient at the recipient's hourly rate.

### 8.3 Eligibility to Receive

Employees facing a qualifying serious medical condition or family emergency, who have exhausted their own PTO and other paid leave benefits, may apply to receive donated PTO. Recipients are limited to 240 hours of donated PTO in a 12-month period.

### 8.4 Application Process

Applications to receive donated PTO must be submitted to Human Resources with appropriate documentation. Approved recipients are not identified to donors, and donations are anonymous unless the donor specifically requests otherwise.

---

## 9\. PTO Recordkeeping

### 9.1 PTO Balance Tracking

Employee PTO balances are tracked through the Company's time and attendance system. Balances are updated each pay period to reflect accruals and use. Employees can view their current PTO balance at any time through the employee self-service portal.

### 9.2 Balance Disputes

Employees who believe their PTO balance is incorrect should contact Human Resources within 30 days of identifying the discrepancy. Adjustments will be made based on supporting documentation.

### 9.3 Fraudulent Use

Misrepresenting the reason for PTO use, falsifying time-off records, or submitting fraudulent PTO requests will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination. The Company reserves the right to require documentation for PTO use, particularly for unscheduled absences.

## 10\. State-Specific Provisions

### 10.1 California PTO Provisions

California law prohibits "use it or lose it" PTO policies. Accrued PTO does not expire and must be paid out at termination. California-mandated paid sick leave is provided separately at one hour per 30 hours worked, with a minimum of 40 hours annually and a usage cap of 40 hours per year. The maximum accrual cap for California employees is 1.5 times the annual accrual rate, after which accrual stops until balance is reduced.

### 10.2 New York PTO Provisions

New York employees receive paid sick leave separately from general PTO at one hour per 30 hours worked, with annual caps of 40 hours for employers with fewer than 100 employees and 56 hours for employers with 100 or more employees. New York City employees may have additional rights under the Earned Safe and Sick Time Act. PTO payout at termination follows the Company's standard practice.

### 10.3 Massachusetts PTO Provisions

Massachusetts earned sick time is provided separately at one hour per 30 hours worked, up to 40 hours annually. Massachusetts requires payout of accrued, unused vacation time at termination, regardless of the reason for separation. Earned sick time is not required to be paid out at termination.

### 10.4 Colorado PTO Provisions

Colorado law prohibits forfeiture of accrued vacation pay. Colorado employees accrue paid sick leave separately at one hour per 30 hours worked, up to 48 hours annually, with public health emergency leave available in addition. All accrued and unused vacation must be paid at termination.

### 10.5 Illinois PTO Provisions

Illinois employees are covered by the Paid Leave for All Workers Act, providing up to 40 hours of paid leave annually accrued at one hour per 40 hours worked. This leave may be used for any reason. Illinois generally requires payout of accrued vacation at termination unless a written policy states otherwise and is consistent with the Illinois Wage Payment and Collection Act.

### 10.6 Washington PTO Provisions

Washington employees accrue paid sick leave separately at one hour per 40 hours worked, with no annual accrual cap but a 40-hour carryover limit. Washington does not require vacation payout at termination unless required by Company policy or contract. The Company's standard payout practice applies.

### 10.7 Other States

In states not specifically listed above, this policy applies as written. The Company complies with all applicable state and local laws regarding paid time off, paid sick leave, and other paid leave entitlements. Employees in any state with questions about state-specific entitlements should contact Human Resources.

## 11\. Policy Administration

### 11.1 Policy Interpretation

Human Resources is responsible for interpreting and administering this policy. Questions about eligibility, accrual, use, or payout should be directed to Human Resources.

### 11.2 Policy Changes

The Company reserves the right to modify this policy at any time, with or without notice, except where prohibited by law. Material changes will be communicated to employees in writing.

### 11.3 Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy may be granted only in writing and only with approval from the Chief Human Resources Officer or designee. Verbal exceptions or commitments are not binding.
