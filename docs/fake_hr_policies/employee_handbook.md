# Employee Handbook

## 1\. Introduction and Welcome

### 1.1 Purpose of This Handbook

This Employee Handbook describes the policies, practices, and benefits that apply to all employees of the Company. It is intended to provide a general overview of expectations and resources available to employees. This handbook does not constitute an employment contract and does not guarantee employment for any specific duration. The Company reserves the right to modify, amend, or terminate any policy at any time, with or without notice, except where prohibited by law.

### 1.2 Equal Employment Opportunity Statement

The Company is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate against any employee or applicant on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, marital status, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by federal, state, or local law. This policy applies to all terms and conditions of employment, including hiring, promotion, termination, compensation, benefits, and training.

### 1.3 At-Will Employment

Employment with the Company is at-will. This means either the employee or the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause and with or without notice, subject to applicable law. No manager, supervisor, or representative of the Company has the authority to enter into any agreement contrary to this at-will relationship except in writing signed by an authorized officer of the Company.

## 2\. Employment Classifications

### 2.1 Full-Time Employees

Full-time employees are those regularly scheduled to work 30 or more hours per week. Full-time employees are eligible for the full range of Company benefits, including health insurance, paid time off, and retirement plan participation, subject to the terms of each benefit plan.

### 2.2 Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees are those regularly scheduled to work fewer than 30 hours per week. Part-time employees may be eligible for limited benefits as described in specific benefit plan documents. Part-time employees accrue paid time off on a prorated basis.

### 2.3 Temporary and Seasonal Employees

Temporary employees are hired for a specific project or limited duration, generally not exceeding six months. Seasonal employees are hired during peak business periods. Neither classification is eligible for Company benefits unless required by law.

### 2.4 Exempt and Non-Exempt Status

Employees are classified as exempt or non-exempt under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA). Non-exempt employees are entitled to overtime pay at one and one-half times their regular rate of pay for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. Exempt employees are not eligible for overtime pay and are paid on a salary basis. Classification is determined based on job duties and salary level, not job title.

### 2.5 Independent Contractors

Independent contractors are not employees of the Company and are not eligible for Company benefits. Independent contractor relationships are governed by separate written agreements.

## 3\. Hiring and Onboarding

### 3.1 Background Checks

All offers of employment are contingent upon the successful completion of a background check, which may include verification of employment history, education, criminal history, and other relevant information. Background checks are conducted in compliance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) and applicable state laws.

### 3.2 Employment Eligibility Verification

All new employees must complete Form I-9 within three business days of their start date to verify their identity and authorization to work in the United States. The Company participates in E-Verify where required by law.

### 3.3 New Hire Orientation

All new employees are required to attend new hire orientation, typically held during the first week of employment. Orientation covers Company policies, benefits enrollment, workplace safety, and required compliance training. Employees must complete all required onboarding tasks within 30 days of their start date.

### 3.4 Introductory Period

The first 90 days of employment are considered an introductory period during which the employee and the Company evaluate fit. This period does not change the at-will nature of employment. Performance feedback is typically provided at the 30, 60, and 90-day marks.

---

## 4\. Compensation and Payroll

### 4.1 Pay Periods and Pay Days

Employees are paid on a bi-weekly basis. Pay periods run from Sunday through the second following Saturday, with payment issued the Friday following the close of the pay period. If a payday falls on a Company holiday, payment will be issued on the preceding business day.

### 4.2 Direct Deposit

Direct deposit is the standard method of payment. Employees may elect to deposit their pay into up to three different accounts. Changes to direct deposit information must be submitted at least five business days before the next payday.

### 4.3 Pay Stubs and Wage Statements

Electronic pay stubs are available through the Company's payroll portal. Employees should review their pay stubs each pay period and report any discrepancies to the Payroll Department within 30 days.

### 4.4 Overtime

Non-exempt employees must obtain advance written approval from their manager before working overtime. Overtime is calculated based on hours actually worked in a workweek and does not include paid time off, holidays, or other paid leave. Working unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action, but the Company will pay for all hours worked.

### 4.5 Payroll Deductions

Standard payroll deductions include federal, state, and local income taxes; Social Security and Medicare taxes; and any voluntary deductions authorized by the employee, such as benefit premiums and retirement contributions. Court-ordered deductions, such as child support garnishments, will be processed in accordance with applicable law.

### 4.6 Compensation Review

Compensation is reviewed annually as part of the performance review process. Salary adjustments are based on individual performance, market conditions, and Company financial performance. Compensation reviews do not guarantee a salary increase.

---

## 5\. Benefits

### 5.1 Health Insurance

The Company offers medical, dental, and vision insurance to eligible full-time employees. Coverage begins on the first of the month following 30 days of employment. Employees may enroll dependents, including spouses, domestic partners, and children up to age 26\. Detailed plan information is provided during enrollment.

### 5.2 Retirement Plan

The Company sponsors a 401(k) retirement plan. Employees are eligible to participate after 60 days of employment. The Company matches employee contributions up to 4% of eligible compensation, with a one-year vesting schedule for the employer match. Employees may contribute up to the IRS annual limit.

### 5.3 Life and Disability Insurance

The Company provides basic life insurance equal to one times annual salary at no cost to the employee. Short-term and long-term disability insurance are also provided. Employees may purchase supplemental life and disability coverage during open enrollment.

### 5.4 Employee Assistance Program

All employees and their household members have access to the Employee Assistance Program (EAP), which provides confidential counseling, legal consultations, financial planning, and referral services at no cost. EAP services are available 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

### 5.5 Open Enrollment

Open enrollment for benefits occurs annually in November. During open enrollment, employees may change their benefit elections for the following plan year. Outside of open enrollment, changes may only be made within 30 days of a qualifying life event, such as marriage, divorce, birth or adoption of a child, or loss of other coverage.

## 6\. Code of Conduct

### 6.1 Professional Behavior

Employees are expected to conduct themselves professionally at all times. This includes treating colleagues, customers, and business partners with respect; communicating constructively; and maintaining a workplace free from disruption. Behavior that creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment will not be tolerated.

### 6.2 Conflicts of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict, or appear to conflict, with the interests of the Company. This includes outside employment, personal relationships with vendors or customers, and financial interests in competitors. Potential conflicts must be disclosed to Human Resources in writing.

### 6.3 Confidentiality

Employees have access to confidential information about the Company, its customers, and its employees. This information must be protected at all times and may not be disclosed to unauthorized parties, used for personal gain, or retained after employment ends. The confidentiality obligation continues after employment terminates.

### 6.4 Use of Company Property

Company property, including computers, phones, vehicles, and office supplies, is provided for business use. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work duties or violate other policies. Employees have no expectation of privacy in their use of Company property or systems.

### 6.5 Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption

The Company prohibits the offer, payment, or acceptance of bribes or kickbacks in any form. Employees must comply with the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and all applicable anti-corruption laws. Gifts and entertainment must be reasonable, infrequent, and disclosed in accordance with the Gift and Entertainment Policy.

## 7\. Workplace Conduct and Anti-Harassment

### 7.1 Prohibition Against Harassment

The Company prohibits harassment of any kind, including harassment based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, marital status, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by law. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and visitors.

### 7.2 Definition of Harassment

Harassment includes unwelcome conduct, whether verbal, physical, or visual, that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment or that interferes with an individual's work performance. Examples include slurs, jokes, derogatory comments, unwanted physical contact, and the display of offensive materials.

### 7.3 Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when submission is made a condition of employment, used as a basis for employment decisions, or has the purpose or effect of creating a hostile work environment.

### 7.4 Reporting Harassment

Any employee who believes they have been subjected to or witnessed harassment should report it immediately to their manager, Human Resources, or through the Company's confidential ethics hotline. Reports may be made verbally or in writing. The Company will investigate all complaints promptly and thoroughly.

### 7.5 Non-Retaliation

The Company strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports harassment, participates in an investigation, or otherwise exercises their rights under this policy. Retaliation is itself a violation of Company policy and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 7.6 Investigation Process

All complaints will be investigated by Human Resources or a designated investigator. The investigation will be conducted as confidentially as possible while ensuring a thorough review. Both the complainant and the respondent will have an opportunity to present their accounts. Findings and any corrective action will be documented.

## 8\. Attendance and Time Off

### 8.1 Regular Attendance

Regular attendance and punctuality are essential to job performance. Employees are expected to be at their workstations and ready to work at their scheduled start time. Excessive absenteeism or tardiness may result in disciplinary action.

### 8.2 Reporting Absences

Employees who will be absent or late must notify their manager as soon as possible, and no later than 30 minutes after their scheduled start time. The notification must include the reason for the absence and the expected return time. Failure to notify may be treated as a no-call, no-show.

### 8.3 No-Call, No-Show

Three consecutive workdays of no-call, no-show absence will be considered job abandonment and voluntary resignation, except where prohibited by law or where the absence is protected by leave law.

### 8.4 Holidays

The Company observes the following paid holidays for full-time employees: New Year's Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Memorial Day, Juneteenth, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving Day, the Friday after Thanksgiving, Christmas Eve, and Christmas Day. When a holiday falls on a Saturday, it is observed on the preceding Friday. When a holiday falls on a Sunday, it is observed on the following Monday.

### 8.5 Bereavement Leave

Employees may take up to five paid days of bereavement leave for the death of an immediate family member, including spouse, domestic partner, parent, child, sibling, grandparent, grandchild, or in-laws of equivalent relation. Up to one paid day is provided for the death of an extended family member.

### 8.6 Jury Duty and Witness Leave

Employees called for jury duty or subpoenaed as a witness will receive paid leave for the duration of their service. Employees must provide a copy of the summons or subpoena to their manager as soon as possible. Employees are expected to return to work on any day they are released early from jury service.

### 8.7 Military Leave

The Company supports employees who serve in the uniformed services. Military leave is provided in accordance with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA). Employees should provide notice of military service obligations as far in advance as possible.

## 9\. Leaves of Absence

### 9.1 Family and Medical Leave

Eligible employees may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) for qualifying reasons, including the birth or adoption of a child, a serious health condition of the employee or a covered family member, or qualifying military exigencies. Employees are eligible after 12 months of employment and 1,250 hours worked in the preceding 12 months.

### 9.2 Personal Leave

Employees who do not qualify for FMLA leave or who have exhausted FMLA leave may request unpaid personal leave for up to 30 days. Personal leave is granted at the Company's discretion based on business needs. Benefits continuation during personal leave is governed by plan documents.

### 9.3 Disability Leave

Employees who are unable to work due to a non-work-related illness or injury may be eligible for short-term disability benefits and job-protected leave under applicable disability accommodation laws. Employees must provide medical certification and follow the Company's leave request procedures.

### 9.4 Pregnancy Accommodations

The Company provides reasonable accommodations to pregnant employees and employees with related medical conditions, including modifications to work duties, schedule adjustments, and additional break time. Accommodation requests should be directed to Human Resources.

### 9.5 Return from Leave

Employees returning from leave will generally be reinstated to their previous position or an equivalent position with equivalent pay, benefits, and other terms of employment. In some cases, this may not be possible due to business circumstances, in which case applicable law will govern.

## 10\. Performance Management

### 10.1 Performance Reviews

Formal performance reviews are conducted annually. Reviews assess job performance, achievement of goals, and development needs. Employees are encouraged to provide a self-assessment and discuss career goals during the review process.

### 10.2 Performance Improvement Plans

Employees whose performance does not meet expectations may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). The PIP outlines specific performance deficiencies, expected improvements, support resources, and a timeline for improvement, typically 30 to 90 days. Failure to meet PIP objectives may result in termination.

### 10.3 Disciplinary Action

The Company uses progressive discipline as appropriate, which may include verbal warnings, written warnings, suspension, and termination. The Company reserves the right to skip any step, including immediate termination, depending on the severity of the conduct.

### 10.4 Promotions and Transfers

Internal promotions and transfers are encouraged. Employees who have completed their introductory period and are in good standing may apply for posted internal positions. Promotion decisions are based on qualifications, performance, and business needs.

## 11\. Workplace Safety and Security

### 11.1 General Safety

The Company is committed to providing a safe workplace. Employees are expected to follow all safety policies, use required protective equipment, and report unsafe conditions to their manager or the Safety Committee immediately.

### 11.2 Workplace Violence

The Company has zero tolerance for violence, threats, intimidation, or weapons in the workplace. Any threat must be reported immediately to Human Resources or Security. Violations will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may be referred to law enforcement.

### 11.3 Drug and Alcohol Policy

The Company maintains a drug-free workplace. The use, possession, sale, or being under the influence of illegal drugs or alcohol during work hours, on Company property, or while conducting Company business is strictly prohibited. The Company conducts drug testing in accordance with applicable law for safety-sensitive positions, post-accident situations, and reasonable suspicion.

### 11.4 Smoking Policy

Smoking, including the use of e-cigarettes and vaping devices, is prohibited inside Company facilities and within 25 feet of building entrances. Designated smoking areas are provided where permitted by law.

### 11.5 Workplace Injuries

Employees must report all work-related injuries or illnesses to their manager immediately, regardless of severity. Workers' compensation benefits are provided in accordance with state law. Failure to report injuries promptly may affect eligibility for benefits.

---

## 12\. Technology and Information Security

### 12.1 Acceptable Use

Company technology resources, including computers, networks, email, and internet access, are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted but must not interfere with work duties, consume excessive resources, or violate other Company policies.

### 12.2 Email and Communication

All Company email and electronic communications are the property of the Company. The Company may monitor, access, and review communications at any time. Employees should have no expectation of privacy in Company communications.

### 12.3 Password and Access Security

Employees must maintain strong passwords, enable multi-factor authentication where available, and never share login credentials. Access to systems and data is granted on a need-to-know basis. Employees must immediately report lost or stolen devices and suspected security incidents.

### 12.4 Social Media

Employees who post about the Company on social media must clearly identify their views as personal and not those of the Company. Confidential information must never be disclosed. Harassment, discrimination, and threats made on social media are subject to the same policies as in-person conduct.

### 12.5 Data Protection

Employees handling personal information of customers, employees, or other individuals must comply with applicable data protection laws and Company data handling procedures. Personal information must be collected, used, stored, and disclosed only for legitimate business purposes.

## 13\. Travel and Expenses

### 13.1 Business Travel

Employees who travel for business must follow the Company's travel policies, including booking through approved channels, complying with class-of-service limits, and submitting timely expense reports. Personal travel may be combined with business travel at the employee's expense.

### 13.2 Expense Reimbursement

Reasonable, necessary, and properly documented business expenses will be reimbursed. Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of incurring the expense and include original receipts for any expense over $25.

### 13.3 Per Diem and Meal Allowances

Per diem rates for meals and incidental expenses follow the General Services Administration (GSA) rates for the travel location. Meals provided by the Company, hosts, or as part of conference fees are not separately reimbursable.

### 13.4 Corporate Credit Cards

Corporate credit cards are issued to employees who travel frequently or have regular business expenses. Cardholders are responsible for following card policies and submitting expense reports promptly. Personal use of corporate cards is prohibited.

---

## 14\. Termination of Employment

### 14.1 Voluntary Resignation

Employees who choose to resign are requested to provide at least two weeks' written notice. Notice should be submitted to the employee's manager and Human Resources. The Company may, at its discretion, accept the resignation effective immediately.

### 14.2 Involuntary Termination

The Company may terminate employment for various reasons, including performance issues, policy violations, misconduct, or business needs. Termination decisions are made in accordance with Company policy and applicable law.

### 14.3 Final Paycheck

Final paychecks will be issued in accordance with state law. Final pay includes wages earned through the last day of employment and any accrued, unused paid time off where required by law or Company policy.

### 14.4 Return of Company Property

Upon termination, employees must return all Company property, including computers, phones, keys, badges, credit cards, documents, and any other items belonging to the Company. Failure to return property may result in deductions from final pay where permitted by law.

### 14.5 Continuation of Benefits

Employees and their covered dependents may be eligible to continue health insurance coverage under COBRA following termination. Information about COBRA rights and other benefit continuation options will be provided at the time of termination.

### 14.6 References

Reference requests for former employees should be directed to Human Resources. The Company's standard practice is to verify dates of employment, position held, and salary. Additional information may be released only with the former employee's written authorization.

## 15\. State-Specific Compliance Addendums

### 15.1 California Addendum

California employees are subject to additional protections under state law. Meal periods of 30 minutes must be provided for shifts exceeding five hours, and a second meal period for shifts exceeding ten hours. Rest breaks of ten minutes are required for every four hours worked. Overtime is calculated daily for hours over eight in a workday and double-time for hours over 12 in a workday. Paid sick leave accrues at one hour for every 30 hours worked, with a minimum of 40 hours annually. California's Family Rights Act (CFRA) provides additional family and medical leave protections beyond FMLA. Final wages must be paid immediately upon involuntary termination and within 72 hours upon voluntary resignation without notice.

### 15.2 New York Addendum

New York employees are entitled to paid sick leave at one hour per 30 hours worked, up to 40 or 56 hours annually depending on employer size. The New York State Paid Family Leave program provides up to 12 weeks of paid leave for qualifying family events. New York City employees are protected under the city's Earned Safe and Sick Time Act and the Fair Workweek Law for certain industries. The state requires sexual harassment prevention training annually for all employees. Final wages must be paid by the next regular payday following termination.

### 15.3 Illinois Addendum

Illinois employees are covered by the Illinois Paid Leave for All Workers Act, which provides up to 40 hours of paid leave annually for any reason. The Illinois Human Rights Act provides broader anti-discrimination protections than federal law, including protection based on arrest record and order of protection status. Illinois requires annual sexual harassment prevention training. The Victims' Economic Security and Safety Act (VESSA) provides leave for employees affected by domestic or sexual violence.

### 15.4 Texas Addendum

Texas does not have a state minimum wage above the federal minimum or state-mandated paid sick leave at the state level. Texas is an employment-at-will state with limited public policy exceptions. Texas employees are eligible for unemployment benefits administered by the Texas Workforce Commission. Final wages must be paid within six days of involuntary termination and by the next regular payday following voluntary resignation. Texas Payday Law governs wage payment requirements.

### 15.5 Washington Addendum

Washington employees accrue paid sick leave at one hour per 40 hours worked, with no annual cap on accrual but a 40-hour annual carryover limit. The Washington Paid Family and Medical Leave program provides up to 12 weeks of paid leave for qualifying events, with extended leave available in certain circumstances. Washington's Equal Pay and Opportunities Act prohibits pay secrecy and requires salary range disclosure in job postings. Final wages must be paid by the next regular payday following termination.

### 15.6 Massachusetts Addendum

Massachusetts employees are entitled to earned sick time at one hour per 30 hours worked, up to 40 hours annually. The Massachusetts Paid Family and Medical Leave program provides up to 26 weeks of combined family and medical leave. The Massachusetts Equal Pay Act prohibits salary history inquiries during hiring. Final wages must be paid on the day of involuntary termination and by the next regular payday following voluntary resignation.

### 15.7 Colorado Addendum

Colorado employees are covered by the Healthy Families and Workplaces Act, providing up to 48 hours of paid sick leave annually plus public health emergency leave. The Colorado Family and Medical Leave Insurance program provides paid family and medical leave benefits. Colorado's Equal Pay for Equal Work Act requires salary range disclosure in job postings and notification of internal promotional opportunities. Colorado prohibits non-compete agreements except for highly compensated workers.

### 15.8 Oregon Addendum

Oregon employees accrue paid sick leave at one hour per 30 hours worked, up to 40 hours annually for employers with ten or more employees. Oregon Paid Leave provides up to 12 weeks of paid family, medical, and safe leave. Oregon's Workplace Fairness Act prohibits non-disclosure agreements covering discrimination, harassment, and retaliation claims. Final wages are due by the end of the next business day following involuntary termination.

Each employee is required to acknowledge receipt of this handbook upon hire and following material updates. The acknowledgment confirms the employee has received the handbook and understands their responsibility to read and comply with its contents.

### 16.2 Policy Updates

This handbook supersedes all prior employee handbooks and inconsistent policies. The Company may update policies at any time and will communicate material changes to employees. Continued employment following the effective date of a policy change constitutes acceptance of the change.

### 16.3 Questions and Clarifications

Employees with questions about handbook policies should contact their manager or Human Resources. The Company encourages open communication and welcomes feedback on policies and procedures.
