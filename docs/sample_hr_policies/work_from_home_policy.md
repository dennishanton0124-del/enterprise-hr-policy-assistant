# Work From Home and Remote Work Policy

**Document ID:** WFH-2025-001 **Policy Area:** Workplace Flexibility **Version:** 4.1 **Effective Date:** January 1, 2025 **Last Reviewed:** December 12, 2024 **Owner:** Human Resources Department

---

## 1\. Policy Overview

### 1.1 Purpose

This policy establishes the framework, expectations, and requirements for employees who work from home or other remote locations on a regular or occasional basis. The Company recognizes that flexible work arrangements can support employee well-being, productivity, and work-life balance while maintaining business performance and team collaboration.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to all employees who work from a location other than a Company office, whether on a full-time remote, hybrid, or occasional basis. The policy covers domestic remote work within the United States. International remote work and out-of-state arrangements require separate approval and may be subject to additional requirements.

### 1.3 Policy Statement

Remote work is a privilege, not an entitlement. Approval for remote work is based on job responsibilities, performance, business needs, and the employee's ability to maintain expected productivity and engagement. The Company reserves the right to modify, suspend, or revoke remote work arrangements at any time based on business needs or performance concerns.

---

## 2\. Remote Work Arrangements

### 2.1 Fully Remote Employees

Fully remote employees work from a designated remote location 100% of the time and have no regular requirement to work from a Company office. Fully remote arrangements are typically established at hire and are designated as remote positions in the job posting and offer letter.

### 2.2 Hybrid Employees

Hybrid employees work from a Company office for part of their schedule and from a remote location for the remainder. Hybrid schedules are typically structured as a minimum number of in-office days per week, with the specific arrangement determined by the employee's manager and team needs.

### 2.3 Occasional Remote Work

Employees whose positions are otherwise office-based may request occasional remote work for specific reasons, such as deep-focus work, personal appointments, mild illness, or weather-related travel concerns. Occasional remote work is approved on a case-by-case basis by the employee's manager.

### 2.4 Temporary Remote Work

Temporary remote work may be approved for limited periods, such as during recovery from a medical procedure, family caregiving needs, or relocation transitions. Temporary remote work arrangements are typically limited to 90 days and require advance approval from Human Resources.

---

## 3\. Eligibility and Approval

### 3.1 Job Suitability

Not all positions are suitable for remote work. Positions involving in-person customer service, hands-on work with physical equipment, secure facility access, or extensive in-person collaboration may not be eligible for remote work. The determination of suitability is made by the Company based on job responsibilities and business needs.

### 3.2 Performance Requirements

Employees must be in good standing and meeting performance expectations to be approved for remote work. Employees on a Performance Improvement Plan or with documented performance issues are generally not eligible for remote work until performance is restored to satisfactory levels.

### 3.3 Tenure Requirements

Employees must complete at least 90 days of employment before requesting hybrid or occasional remote work, except where the position is designated as fully remote at hire. New hires in remote-eligible positions may be required to work in-office during initial training and onboarding.

### 3.4 Approval Process

Requests for ongoing remote work arrangements must be submitted in writing to the employee's manager and reviewed in consultation with Human Resources. Approvals are documented in a Remote Work Agreement signed by the employee, manager, and Human Resources representative.

### 3.5 Reassessment and Renewal

Remote work arrangements are reviewed annually as part of the performance review process. Arrangements may be modified or discontinued based on changes in job responsibilities, performance, business needs, or other factors. Employees will be given reasonable notice of changes whenever possible.

---

## 4\. Work Hours and Availability

### 4.1 Standard Work Hours

Remote employees are expected to work their standard scheduled hours unless otherwise agreed in writing with their manager. Core collaboration hours, during which employees must be available for meetings and real-time communication, are typically 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM local time of the employee's manager, but may vary by team.

### 4.2 Time Zone Considerations

Employees working from a time zone different from their team must coordinate with their manager to establish working hours that provide sufficient overlap with team core hours. Time zone differences greater than three hours may require schedule adjustments to ensure adequate collaboration.

### 4.3 Availability Expectations

Remote employees must be available and responsive during scheduled work hours. Response times to communications should be consistent with in-office expectations: instant messages within 30 minutes during work hours, emails within four business hours, and meeting requests within one business day.

### 4.4 Time Tracking

Non-exempt remote employees must accurately record all hours worked, including meal periods and rest breaks where required by state law. Time records must be submitted by the end of each pay period. Working off-the-clock is prohibited and may result in disciplinary action.

### 4.5 Overtime

Non-exempt remote employees must obtain advance approval before working overtime. Working unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action, but the Company will pay for all hours worked. Working outside of scheduled hours, including evenings and weekends, requires explicit manager approval.

## 5\. Workspace Requirements

### 5.1 Designated Work Area

Remote employees must maintain a designated work area that is suitable for performing job duties. The work area should be quiet, well-lit, ergonomically appropriate, and free from significant distractions during work hours. Working from public locations such as coffee shops or libraries is permitted only on an occasional basis and is not appropriate for handling confidential information.

### 5.2 Internet and Connectivity

Remote employees must maintain a reliable, high-speed internet connection capable of supporting video conferencing, file transfers, and other business applications. Minimum internet speeds of 25 Mbps download and 5 Mbps upload are required. The Company does not reimburse the cost of personal internet service except where required by state law.

### 5.3 Ergonomics and Safety

Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe and ergonomically appropriate work environment. The Company provides general ergonomic guidance through Human Resources. Employees experiencing ergonomic concerns should contact Human Resources to discuss accommodations.

### 5.4 Workspace Photos

Employees may be asked to provide photographs of their designated work area as part of the remote work approval process. Photos are used to confirm workspace suitability and are kept confidential.

### 5.5 Family and Caregiving Responsibilities

Remote work is not a substitute for childcare, eldercare, or other dependent care arrangements. Employees must have appropriate care arrangements in place during scheduled work hours. Limited interruptions for routine family matters are expected and acceptable, but ongoing caregiving responsibilities during work hours are not.

## 6\. Equipment and Technology

### 6.1 Company-Provided Equipment

The Company provides remote employees with a standard equipment package, which typically includes a laptop computer, monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset, and webcam. Equipment is shipped to the employee's designated remote work location at the Company's expense.

### 6.2 Equipment Stipend

In lieu of certain Company-provided equipment, eligible remote employees may receive a one-time home office setup stipend of up to $500. The stipend may be used for ergonomic furniture, lighting, additional monitors, or similar items. Stipend purchases require receipts and are reimbursed through the standard expense report process.

### 6.3 Use of Personal Equipment

Remote employees should generally use Company-provided equipment for all work activities. Use of personal computers or devices for Company work is permitted only with manager approval and only when accessing the Company network through approved methods such as a virtual private network (VPN) and Company applications.

### 6.4 Equipment Maintenance and Repair

The Company is responsible for maintaining and repairing Company-provided equipment. Employees experiencing equipment issues should contact the IT Help Desk for support. Damaged equipment must be reported promptly. Employees may be responsible for the cost of repairing or replacing equipment damaged through negligence or misuse.

### 6.5 Return of Equipment

Upon termination of employment, change in role, or end of remote work arrangement, employees must return all Company-provided equipment within ten business days. The Company will provide return shipping materials and instructions. Equipment not returned may be deducted from final pay where permitted by law.

---

## 7\. Information Security and Confidentiality

### 7.1 Network Security

Remote employees must connect to Company resources only through approved methods, including the Company VPN and approved cloud applications. Use of public Wi-Fi networks for Company work is prohibited unless connected through the Company VPN. Home networks must be password-protected with strong encryption.

### 7.2 Device Security

Company-provided devices must be locked when unattended. Employees must enable full-disk encryption, automatic updates, and approved endpoint security software at all times. Personal use of Company devices must comply with the Acceptable Use Policy.

### 7.3 Confidential Information

Confidential and proprietary information must be protected at all times when working remotely. Confidential information should not be displayed on screens visible to non-employees, discussed in public locations, or stored on personal devices or cloud services. Documents should be stored only in approved Company systems.

### 7.4 Printing and Physical Documents

Printing of confidential documents at home should be minimized. Printed confidential documents must be securely stored when not in use and shredded when no longer needed. Employees should not maintain personal copies of Company documents.

### 7.5 Family Member Access

Family members and other household members must not have access to Company devices, systems, or confidential information. Employees should ensure that workspaces are private during meetings and calls involving sensitive information.

### 7.6 Reporting Security Incidents

Suspected security incidents, including lost or stolen devices, suspicious emails, unauthorized access, or accidental disclosure of confidential information, must be reported to the IT Security team immediately, regardless of whether the incident occurred during work hours.

## 8\. Communication and Collaboration

### 8.1 Communication Tools

Remote employees are expected to use Company-approved communication tools, including email, instant messaging, video conferencing, and project management platforms. Personal communication tools, social media, or messaging apps should not be used for business communications.

### 8.2 Video Conferencing

Cameras should generally be on during team meetings and meetings with managers, customers, or external parties, unless there is a specific reason to keep cameras off. Employees should be appropriately dressed and have a professional background or use a virtual background during video meetings.

### 8.3 Meeting Participation

Remote employees are expected to actively participate in meetings, including contributing to discussions, asking questions, and following up on action items. Meetings should be conducted from the employee's designated workspace, not from cars, public locations, or while engaged in other activities.

### 8.4 Communication Cadence

Remote employees should maintain regular communication with their manager and team through scheduled one-on-one meetings, team meetings, and informal check-ins. Managers are responsible for establishing communication norms with their remote team members.

### 8.5 In-Person Gatherings

The Company periodically hosts in-person team meetings, training sessions, and Company events. Remote employees may be expected to attend in-person events at Company offices or other locations, with reasonable notice and with travel expenses covered by the Company.

## 9\. Tax, Legal, and Location Considerations

### 9.1 Designated Work Location

Employees must work from the location designated in their Remote Work Agreement. Working from a different state or country requires advance written approval from Human Resources due to tax, legal, and compliance implications.

### 9.2 State Tax Implications

Working from a state different from the Company's primary place of business may create state tax obligations for both the employee and the Company. The Company may not be registered to do business or withhold taxes in all states. Employees who relocate or wish to work from a different state must notify Human Resources at least 60 days in advance.

### 9.3 International Remote Work

Working internationally is generally not permitted. Limited exceptions may be approved for short-duration travel of less than 30 days in a calendar year. International remote work creates significant tax, immigration, and compliance issues that require advance review by Legal and Human Resources.

### 9.4 Workers' Compensation

Workers' compensation insurance covers employees while performing job duties at their designated remote work location. Employees must report any work-related injuries occurring at their remote workspace immediately, following the same reporting procedures as office-based injuries.

### 9.5 Expense Reimbursement

In states that require reimbursement of business expenses incurred while working from home, the Company provides a monthly stipend to cover a reasonable portion of internet, phone, and utility costs. Specific reimbursement amounts are determined by state law and Company policy.

## 10\. Performance Management

### 10.1 Performance Standards

Remote employees are held to the same performance standards as in-office employees. Performance is evaluated based on results, not hours worked or visibility. Managers are expected to establish clear goals, provide regular feedback, and assess performance objectively.

### 10.2 Productivity Monitoring

The Company does not engage in invasive productivity monitoring such as keystroke logging, webcam surveillance, or similar tools for general remote workforce management. The Company may use standard business tools that capture activity within approved applications, consistent with the Acceptable Use Policy.

### 10.3 Performance Issues

Performance concerns identified for remote employees are addressed through the same performance management process as in-office employees, including coaching, performance improvement plans, and disciplinary action. Persistent performance issues may result in revocation of the remote work arrangement.

### 10.4 Engagement and Inclusion

Managers are responsible for ensuring that remote employees are included in team activities, decision-making, and career development opportunities. Remote employees should not be disadvantaged in promotion, training, or visibility compared to in-office colleagues.

---

## 11\. Modifications and Termination of Arrangement

### 11.1 Voluntary Changes

Employees who wish to modify their remote work arrangement should submit a request to their manager. Requests are evaluated based on business needs and the same criteria used for initial approval. Approved modifications are documented in an updated Remote Work Agreement.

### 11.2 Company-Initiated Changes

The Company may modify or terminate remote work arrangements at any time based on business needs, performance, or policy changes. Reasonable notice will be provided whenever possible, typically at least 30 days for material changes such as a return-to-office requirement.

### 11.3 Performance-Based Revocation

Remote work arrangements may be revoked due to performance issues, policy violations, or failure to meet remote work expectations. Revocation may occur with limited notice and may require the employee to begin reporting to a Company office.

### 11.4 Return-to-Office Transitions

Employees transitioning from remote to in-office work will be given reasonable time to make arrangements, typically at least 30 days. Relocation assistance is generally not provided for return-to-office transitions, but exceptions may be considered on a case-by-case basis.

---

## 12\. Special Circumstances

### 12.1 Inclement Weather and Office Closures

When Company offices are closed due to inclement weather or other emergencies, in-office and hybrid employees are generally expected to work from home if able. Office closures do not automatically result in paid time off; employees who cannot work due to closures should follow standard time-off request procedures.

### 12.2 Public Health Events

During declared public health emergencies, the Company may implement temporary expanded remote work policies for the safety of employees and the community. Such temporary policies are communicated separately and supersede this policy for the duration of the emergency.

### 12.3 Disability Accommodations

Employees with disabilities may request remote work as a reasonable accommodation under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) or applicable state law. Accommodation requests are evaluated through the interactive process facilitated by Human Resources and are not subject to the standard remote work eligibility criteria.

### 12.4 Caregiver Accommodations

Employees with significant short-term caregiving responsibilities may request temporary remote work arrangements. Such requests are evaluated case-by-case in coordination with applicable leave policies and family-care benefits.

## 13\. State-Specific Provisions

### 13.1 California Remote Work Provisions

California requires reimbursement of necessary business expenses incurred by employees, including a portion of home internet, phone, and utility costs when working remotely. The Company provides eligible California-based remote employees with a monthly stipend of $75 to cover such expenses. California meal and rest break requirements apply to remote employees, who must record their breaks accurately in the Company time system.

### 13.2 Illinois Remote Work Provisions

Illinois requires reimbursement of necessary expenses incurred by employees while working from home. Eligible Illinois-based remote employees receive a monthly expense stipend of $50. Illinois employees must comply with the One Day Rest in Seven Act, which guarantees at least 24 consecutive hours of rest per calendar week.

### 13.3 Massachusetts Remote Work Provisions

Massachusetts does not require expense reimbursement for remote work but does require employers to ensure compliance with applicable wage and hour laws for remote workers. The Company provides Massachusetts-based remote employees with the standard equipment package and home office setup stipend.

### 13.4 New York Remote Work Provisions

New York employees working remotely are subject to the same wage notice, paid sick leave, and other state law protections as in-office employees. New York City employees may have additional rights under city-specific ordinances. The Company complies with all applicable state and city requirements for remote workers.

### 13.5 Washington Remote Work Provisions

Washington requires reimbursement of necessary business expenses, including a reasonable portion of internet and phone costs for remote work. Eligible Washington-based remote employees receive a monthly stipend of $50. Washington's salary range disclosure requirements apply to all positions, including fully remote positions, regardless of where the employee is located.

### 13.6 Other States

In states not specifically listed, this policy applies as written, supplemented by applicable state and local requirements. Employees with questions about state-specific provisions should contact Human Resources.

---

## 14\. Policy Compliance

### 14.1 Policy Acknowledgment

Employees approved for remote work must acknowledge this policy and sign a Remote Work Agreement before beginning the arrangement. The acknowledgment confirms the employee has read and understands the policy requirements.

### 14.2 Policy Violations

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of the remote work arrangement and termination of employment. Violations include failure to maintain workspace standards, security breaches, unauthorized work location changes, and failure to meet availability or performance expectations.

### 14.3 Policy Updates

The Company reserves the right to modify this policy at any time, with or without notice, except where prohibited by law. Material changes will be communicated to remote employees in writing and may require an updated Remote Work Agreement.

### 14.4 Contact Information

Questions about this policy should be directed to Human Resources or the employee's manager. Specific questions about technology, equipment, or security should be directed to the IT Help Desk.
